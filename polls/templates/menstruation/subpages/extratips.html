Tracking Your Period

Track your period manually or with an app, so you can predict when you need to be ready for your next time. Apps are helpful if your period is irregular especially during the first year of your period, as it finds trends to help make the predictions. Flo is a useful, free app avaliable on ios and android.

Getting Your Period in Public

If your period starts in public and you don’t have a product on hand, do not be scared to ask someone for a pad or tampon. Chances are the same thing has happened to them, and they will be totally willing to give you one.

Removal of Period Blood Stains

If unwanted period blood leaks on your clothes, easily get blood stains out with cold water and soap. The sooner the stains are treated, the easier they will be to remove.

Disposing Used Period Products

Don’t flush pads or tampons down the toilet. Place them in the garbage can next to the toilet or in the little trash box provided in most public bathroom stalls. Pads and tampons can end up clogging toilets. if flushed.

Don't be Embarrassed

There is no need to feel embarrassed about your period. It is a normal bodily process and about half of the population has, is going to, or is experiencing the same thing you are.
